Stop Word: Suspicious Cosmetic Ingredients

January 22, 2021

Gone are the days when girls bought beautiful jars with the words "eco-bio-organic" for a beautiful photo on Instagram. There is no more excitement around natural cosmetics. A different era has come when it is no longer necessary to loudly and colorfully expose the sins of paraben-glycol creams. The girls grew up and realized that the main thing is not a jar, but its contents, that cunning manufacturers have a reason to skillfully disguise toxic components under beautiful names and labels (yes, under this very "eco-bio"), that there is a reason to carefully study the composition of the cream or masks before trying them on yourself. And there are at least five reasons to choose natural cosmetics instead of regular ones.

Reason one: pharmaceutical

Most of the documents regulating cosmetic production still describe cosmetics as products that do not penetrate deeper than the stratum corneum of the skin. Old textbooks on cosmetology and dermatology use the thesis about the impermeability of the skin, approve the use of chemicals in the composition of cosmetics with a large size of molecules that "lie on the surface and are not absorbed", in other words, create an occlusive film. This approach was formed in the middle of the twentieth century and, unfortunately, has not yet been revised at the legislative level, and given how many cosmetic corporations are tied in their industries on this very approach, it is unlikely that they will gather to lobby for the revision of official papers in the near future.

But the accumulated scientific data over the past 10-15 years has forced a serious rethink of the issue of skin permeability, and this happened thanks to pharmacists. Today, there are more and more medicinal transdermal drugs, those that affect the body by penetrating through the skin. For example, popular nicotine patches, patches with a powerful painkiller, which are used even for cancer patients, patches-contraceptives. And if you add to this the fact that, according to research, even petroleum products with their large molecules, applied to the skin, after a couple of hours form a kind of "depot", through which they still penetrate into the deep layers of the skin and then into the bloodstream. The theory of the impenetrability of the skin begins to crack at the seams.
